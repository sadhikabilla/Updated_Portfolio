ANAKIN

Winner of the 8th Computing Student Project Showcase (STePS)


Anakin is an app that connects busy dog owners with dog walkers around the vicinity. You can either sign up as a dog walker or request for a walker around the area! Check it out on the App Store!


Prototype


The Problem


Studies have found that one in five dog owners are too busy to give their pets an everyday walk. It can be pretty difficult for dog owners to find trusted dog walkers on demand. To address this problem, my team and I created Anakin - an app that lets busy dog owners find dog walkers, schedule walks and track their dogs while being walked.


User Study


To analyse the problem we conducted interviews and contextual inquiry with both dog owners and dog walkers. Key observations were summarized in flow models. Some of the conclusions that we drew from the process of contextual enquiry were: Trust between the walker and dog owner is essential, dogs have different needs and personalities and there needs to be some way for dog owners to communicate their preferences to the walkers, owners want to know what their pets are doing on their walk and reviews are a good way to gauge the quality of dogwalkers. We summarized these finding using flow models.

Once we completed the interviews and created the personas, we met up to discuss the basic flow of the app and the important features that we wanted to have. We then put all the storyboards together to visualise the flow of the entire app before creating the prototype.

VAMP

VAMP received a SGD$10,000 seed grant from NUS Enterprise


VAMP, a startup idea that I, along with two others, pitched to NUS Enterprise, aims to reduce the consumption of vampire energy. VAMP received a seed grant of 10k SGD and is under development!.


The problem


It's alarming to note that 30% of your electricity bill goes in vampire energy - energy that's being consumed but not put to any good use. We wanted to find a way to be able to solve this problem which led to the creation of VAMP, a smart IoT device that saves you money, by conserving vampire energy.


Overview of the solution



Research and needfinding


We started off the process with premilinary research to understand how big of a problem this actually is. Through our research we were able to get statistics on how much of an electricity bill goes in vampire energy (30%), how much vampire energy is wasted in an year (210 Quadrillion BTU) and the monetary value of this wastage ($5525 Billion). We then went further and conducted interviews with individuals, college residences (College of Alice and Peter Tan) and co-working spaces like WeWork, Alley NYC and ERA NYC. We collected their main pain points and came up with certain essential features that we needed to mitigate these pain points. following which, we created a value proposition canvas.

Competitor Analysis


Before we began designing our product, we looked at all the existing products and did a thorough analysis to make sure we would stand out and offer a much better experience. It also helped us understand what 'not to do' in order for our product to be successful.

Hardware prototyping



I experimented with several designs and quickly sketched them out before landing on the one below. Creating sketches was a quick way for me to share my ideas with the rest of the team.

Once we decided on a particular design, I used rhino (with the help of my friend) to render it (my first attempt at using rhino). The hardware is designed to be simple, portable and can be connected to multiple devices.

The hardware is designed to be simple, portable and sleek. Once the design for the hardware was complete, I began creating the app. The goal in our case was to make it as simple as possible for our users to save money by automatically turning off their appliances, without them having to go through multiple steps.


Storyboarding


I started off by storyboarding and making simple sketches of the interface, which I then converted into a high fidelity prototype. The screens were made using Illustrator.

Prototype


Once both the storyboard and the low fidelity prototype were complete, I started working on the interface for the app. The app is able to detect when the users phone is at a distance of more than five miles from the appliance for a certain amount of time (set by the user). The app then sends a notification alerting the user of an appliance that is turned on or has been left in the sleep mode. Users can either switch off the devices manually or choose to have them automatically turn off. I'm still working on building the app. In the meantime, click around to interact with the prototype!

FIIND MII

Fiind Mii is our solution for a seamless photo discovery and photo sharing experience. Fiind Mii aims to create a smarter way to discover photos relevant to you and connect photographers to their photo subjects.


Prototype


This is our way of changing the would of photography! Play around with the prototype below.


The problem


Photographs serve as a valuable purpose of creating memories. But let’s think in the case of large-scale events - conferences, weddings, conventions. There are hundreds or even thousands of people, with photographers sweeping through the crowd for shots. On occasions like this, you’re overwhelmed with a flood of photos, when really, you’re only interested in just the ones you’re in. Photographers also face frustrations but for a different reason. Photographers - professional or amateur - want their work to be appreciated. And who else better to appreciate their work than the people they have photographed? We wanted to find a way to connect photographers to the people they photograph!


User Study


We started off by conducting interviews with bloggers, photographers and individuals who frequent various events. Through our interviews we found that our potential target market is huge. Conferences, concerts, hackathons, comic-cons, fashion week etc. are events with a large number of people and photographers. In Singapore alone, we see big events such as the annual Standard Chartered Marathon, NDP, Colour Run, Pink Dot SG, with high participation numbers. Attendees range from over 9,000 a year and people want to document their time and these events.


Preliminary sketches



Preliminary sketches were made before designing the actual interface.

Creating the app

Fiind Mii makes use of location tracking technologies such as GPS, and WiFi triangulation, to let you discover photos of yourself instantly. Photographers can upload their photos with vital information like timestamps and location. Users can then, using their location history, figure out which photos they are in by virtue of proximity. I'm currently working on coding out the app!


My role


I was responsible for designing the screens and assited in counducting the user study. As the sole developer in the team, I was responsible for coding out the app as well.

NUS PROJECT ADMINISTRATION SYSTEM REDESIGN

The NUS SIP (Student Internship Portal) is an application portal that allows students in NUS to submit applications for a summer or six month internship, which they need to complete as a part of their graduation requirements. This is a paid project is in collaboration with the National University Of Singapore


Prototype


CLICK HERE TO INTERACT WITH THE XD PROTOTYPE


The Problem


The current NUS ATAP portal lacks successful usability and branding. The task given to me was to redesign the portal and improve both the asthetic feel as well as the end-to-end experience of the website for both students as well as companies.


User Group


The portal is primarily used by students from the NUS School of Computing. I identified two potential user groups - students who have used the current portal and those who are new to it.

User Study


To understand pain points, I approached 3rd and 4th year students from the School Of Computing (SoC) who had used the portal before to secure internships as well as first year students who were new to the portal. I conducted contextual enquiry through interviews and with the help of my school, sent out surveys to students to get a better understanding of their expections from the portal.


User Journey


The user journey of most students can be divided into the following steps -


Personas

By consolidating what I learnt from the user study to consolidate the needs, goals and behaviours of the user groups. These personas are created from real users.


Design Process


Based on the feedback and information collected, I defined 3 main issues that I wanted the improved portal to address:

  • 1) Navigation
  • 2) Resume Upload
  • 3) Project Selection

I then divsed four key teaks to resolve these issues -

  • 1) Consolidate the information page and make navigation between the SIP/ATAP sites more streamlined
  • 2) Work on Resume creation and submission
  • 3) Viewing Interview details and application status
  • 4) improving internship selection by allowing user to view, filter, compare and rank their choices.
